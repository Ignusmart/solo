## Executive Summary

ScopeShield has a real pain point but faces a brutal competitive reality that was underestimated in the original scoring. **The name "ScopeShield" is already taken** by an existing product (scopeshield.cloud, built by Valynx, launched Feb 2026). ## 2. Market Reality: Real Pain, But Do They PAY to Solve It?

### The pain is real and well-documented:
- 52% of projects experience scope creep (PMI Pulse of the Profession)
- 57% of agencies lose $1K-5K/month to unbilled scope creep
- 80%+ of freelancers report regular scope creep
- Average freelancer loses $2K-5K/year in unpaid work (conservative estimate)
- Some sources cite $15K-25K/year for established freelancers

### But the solution freelancers actually use is... contracts and conversations

Every single piece of advice from experienced freelancers boils down to:
1. **Write better contracts** -- detailed scope, revision limits (2 rounds standard), kill fees (25-50%), explicit exclusions
2. **Use a change order process** -- "Sure, that's an additional $X. Want me to proceed?"
3. **Track time** -- tag tasks as #planned vs #unplanned
4. **Address it immediately** -- don't absorb it silently

The dominant solution is behavioral/contractual, not software. Freelancers who solve scope creep do it with a better contract template and the confidence to say "that's out of scope." The ones who don't solve it have a psychological barrier to confrontation -- and software doesn't fix that.

### The spreadsheet/free-tool trap

Freelancers already have:
- Contract templates from Bonsai, AND CO, or legal template sites
- Time tracking in Toggl, Harvest, or Clockify (free)
- Project management in Notion, Trello, or ClickUp (free)
- Invoicing in Wave (free) or Stripe

A dedicated scope-creep tool sits awkwardly between tools they already use.

## 3. The "Just Use a Contract" Objection

This is the strongest kill signal. The standard advice for scope creep prevention is:

> "Instead of 'Build a website,' write: '5-page responsive website (Home, About, Services, Portfolio, Contact) with contact form integration -- excludes e-commerce, custom animations, and copywriting.' Include 2 rounds of revisions. Charge hourly rate + 10-20% nuisance fee for out-of-scope work."

Experienced freelancers universally recommend:
- Detailed scope of work with explicit **Exclusions** section
- Revision limits baked into the contract (2 rounds industry standard)
- Kill fee clause (25-50% of project value)
- Formal change request process in the contract language itself

The contract IS the scope-creep tool. A well-written contract with a confident freelancer who enforces it makes a dedicated scope-tracking tool redundant.

**Counter-argument**: New freelancers don't know how to write good contracts. True, but they can get Bonsai templates for free or pay $17/mo for the full Bonsai suite that includes contracts + invoicing + time tracking + project management.

## 7. AI Moat Reality: Tissue-Thin

### What "AI scope analysis" actually means:
Based on the existing ScopeShield (Valynx) and ScopePilot:
1. Upload/paste contract text
2. Forward client email with new request
3. GPT/Claude compares request against contract scope
4. Returns "in scope" or "out of scope" verdict with cited clause

### This is a GPT wrapper. Specifically:
- Take contract text -> chunk it -> embed or stuff into context
- Take client request -> compare against contract
- Return classification + relevant clause

Any competent developer can build this in a weekend. The "AI analysis" is a single prompt:

```
Given this contract scope: {scope}
And this client request: {request}
Is the request within scope? Cite the relevant clause.
```

### Defensibility: Near zero
- No proprietary data moat (each user's contracts are unique, don't compound)
- No network effects
- Foundation model improvements make this easier, not harder, to replicate
- Bonsai, Plutio, or HoneyBook could add this as a feature with one sprint
- Any freelancer can paste their contract + client email into ChatGPT and ask the same question for free

### The "just use ChatGPT" test:
A freelancer can literally open ChatGPT, paste their contract, paste the client's email, and ask "Is this in scope?" This works TODAY, for free, with zero onboarding.

## 8. Churn Risk: Catastrophic for Unit Economics

### Benchmark churn for SMB/freelancer SaaS:
- SMB SaaS: **3-7% monthly churn** (industry benchmarks)
- Marketing/sales tools (closest category): **4.8-8.1% monthly churn**
- Freelancer-targeted tools specifically: Higher end due to budget sensitivity, business volatility, low switching costs

### Realistic scenario at 5% monthly churn:
- Average customer lifetime: 20 months
- At $29/mo: LTV = $580
- At $15/mo (realistic price): LTV = $300
- Need CAC < $100 to be viable, which is extremely difficult for a niche B2B product

### Seasonal/project churn:
Freelancers subscribe when they have a problematic client, then cancel when the project ends. Scope creep is episodic, not continuous. This creates brutal churn dynamics -- users get value from a single project and leave.

## 9. TAM Reality Check

### The funnel:
1. **72M+ freelancers in the US** (total, including gig workers, part-timers)
2. **~30M do project-based work** (not Uber drivers or DoorDash)
3. **~23% use any PM software** -> ~7M using tools
4. **80% experience scope creep** -> ~5.6M have the problem
5. **But most solve it with contracts/conversations** -> Maybe 20% would consider software -> ~1.1M
6. **Willingness to pay for a DEDICATED tool** (not a feature in their CRM) -> optimistically 5% -> ~55K
7. **Actually discover and trial the product** -> 2-5% conversion -> ~1,100-2,750 paying users

### Revenue ceiling:
- At $15/mo (realistic price) x 2,000 users = $30K MRR = $360K ARR
- At $29/mo (proposed price) x 1,000 users = $29K MRR = $348K ARR
- Getting to 1,000-2,000 paying users as a solo founder takes 12-24 months minimum

### The $2K MRR target:
- At $15/mo: need 134 paying users
- At $29/mo: need 69 paying users
- Achievable in theory but requires 6-12 months with aggressive distribution, not 6 months as originally estimated
