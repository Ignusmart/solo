## Three Ideas Analyzed in Depth

### Idea 1: DeadlineGuard — AI Contract & Deadline Tracker for Small Businesses

**Problem**: Small businesses manage 15-50+ contracts, subscriptions, licenses, and permits — each with renewal dates, notice periods, and cancellation windows. 67% of businesses don't track renewal dates at all. The average mid-sized company loses $15,000/year to forgotten renewals and zombie subscriptions. Missing a renewal by 48 hours locks you into another 12 months. Missing a cancellation window forces premium monthly billing at 2x the annual rate. One construction company forgot to renew a contractor's license, got shut down for 2 weeks, lost $15,000 in fines and 3 jobs.

The pain is not just SaaS subscriptions (which Termedora and Stitchflow cover). It's the FULL spectrum: vendor contracts, insurance policies, business licenses, software subscriptions, lease agreements, certifications, domain renewals, warranty expirations. No single tool covers this breadth for small businesses at an affordable price.

**Who has it**: Small business owners (1-50 employees) managing a mix of software subscriptions, vendor contracts, insurance renewals, business licenses, and leases. Operations managers, office managers, and founders who are the "person who knows when stuff expires."

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **Termedora** ($49/mo) is purpose-built for SaaS contract renewals specifically. Good for IT/ops teams tracking vendor software. Doesn't cover insurance, licenses, leases, certifications.
- **ExpiryEdge** is the closest competitor — tracks licenses, insurance, contracts, certs. Free plan available. But: no AI extraction (manual data entry), limited reporting, and relatively new/unproven.
- **Stitchflow** is free but email-only, SaaS-focused, thin feature set.
- **Contract Hound** ($95/mo) is mid-market pricing — too expensive for small businesses managing 20 contracts.
- **Google Calendar / spreadsheets** — the real competitor. 67% of businesses use manual tracking. Falls apart at scale, no notice-period logic, no team visibility.
- **Gap**: No tool (a) lets you upload ANY contract/document and AI-extracts key dates, notice periods, and terms, (b) covers the FULL spectrum (SaaS + insurance + licenses + leases + certs), (c) sends smart reminders with the right lead time (not just "30 days before" but "your 90-day notice period starts April 1 — act by then or you're locked in"), and (d) prices at $19-39/mo for small businesses.

**MVP scope (2 weeks)**:
1. Upload a contract PDF or image → AI extracts: counterparty, contract type, start date, end date, notice period, auto-renewal clause, $ value.
2. Dashboard showing all deadlines in a timeline view: "3 renewals this month, 1 license expiring, 2 in notice period NOW."
3. Smart reminders: email + Slack + SMS at configurable intervals. Key difference: reminders based on notice period, not just expiry date ("You have 14 days to cancel before auto-renewal — act by April 15").
4. Team sharing: invite team members, assign contracts to owners.
5. Stripe billing.

**Revenue model**: Free tier (5 contracts, email reminders only). Pro $29/mo (unlimited contracts, AI extraction, Slack/SMS, team sharing). Business $59/mo (API access, bulk import, audit log, priority support).

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $5K-$15K MRR. The pain is financial and universal — every business has contracts that expire. The ROI story is immediate: "DeadlineGuard saved us $12,000 by catching a notice period we would have missed." But: the competitive landscape is more active than it looks. ExpiryEdge, Termedora, and even simple tools like Stitchflow nibble at pieces of this. The differentiation is breadth (not just SaaS) + AI extraction (not manual entry) + smart notice-period logic.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: AI/LLM for document extraction and date parsing. Full-stack Next.js for dashboard. API design for integrations. Python for the extraction pipeline. Can leverage Claude's document understanding for contract parsing — genuinely better than keyword extraction.

**Kill criteria**: If < 100 signups in 60 days, or if users say "ExpiryEdge already does this" (must verify ExpiryEdge's actual product depth vs. marketing). Also kill if AI extraction accuracy < 85% on real contracts (hallucinated dates = trust destruction).

### Idea 2: HireKit — Lightweight Structured Hiring Toolkit for Startups (Scorecards + Scheduling + Debrief)

**Problem**: Startups making their first 5-20 hires don't need an ATS. They need three things: (1) structured interview scorecards so every interviewer evaluates the same criteria, (2) scheduling that doesn't require 15 emails, and (3) a debrief space where interviewers share feedback before the hiring meeting. Today, founders use Google Docs for scorecards (no structure), email for scheduling (chaos), and Slack for debriefs (feedback gets lost). The result: 74% of companies admit they've hired the wrong person. Bad hires cost 30% of first-year salary.

Enterprise ATS tools (Greenhouse $6K+/year, Lever, Ashby) solve all this but are overkill and overpriced for a 10-person startup hiring 2 people this quarter. Free tools like HireLikeaPro exist but are single-player (no team collaboration, no scheduling, no debrief workflow — it's an AI scorecard generator, not a hiring workflow tool).

**Who has it**: Startup founders and first-time hiring managers at companies with 5-50 employees, hiring 1-5 people per quarter. They don't have an HR person. The founder or eng lead is running hiring alongside product work.

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **Greenhouse/Lever/Ashby** ($500-6K+/year): Enterprise ATS. Way too complex and expensive for "we're hiring 2 engineers this quarter." Structured hiring is there but buried under pipeline management, CRM, and analytics features startups don't need.
- **HireLikeaPro** (free): AI scorecard generator. Single-player tool — generates a scorecard and analyzes transcripts. No team collaboration, no scheduling, no shared debrief. The founder uses it alone; interviewers still email their feedback.
- **Notion templates**: Popular but unstructured. No enforcement of scoring criteria, no aggregate scores, no debrief workflow. A blank canvas that requires discipline to maintain.
- **Google Forms for feedback**: Works but ugly, no aggregate view, no comparison across candidates, interviewers can see each other's scores (biasing), and feedback is divorced from the candidate profile.
- **Gap**: A purpose-built tool that gives a 10-person startup structured hiring in 5 minutes: create a role → generate scorecard → share interview links with your team → each interviewer scores independently (can't see others' scores) → debrief view shows aggregate scores and highlights disagreements → hire/no-hire recommendation. No pipeline management, no CRM, no sourcing. Just the decision-quality layer.

**MVP scope (2 weeks)**:
1. Create a role: title + 3-5 key competencies (AI suggests from job description).
2. AI generates structured scorecard with behavioral questions and 1-5 rating scale per competency.
3. Add candidates (name + resume). Add interviewers (email invite).
4. Each interviewer gets a unique link → fills out scorecard after interview. Scores are hidden from other interviewers (anti-bias).
5. Debrief dashboard: side-by-side candidate comparison, aggregate scores, flag where interviewers disagree by 2+ points, AI-generated summary of strengths/concerns.
6. Calendar integration for scheduling (Google Calendar API).
7. Stripe billing.

**Revenue model**: Free tier (1 active role, 3 candidates, 2 interviewers). Pro $39/mo (unlimited roles, unlimited candidates, 10 interviewers, AI scorecard generation, calendar sync). Team $79/mo (unlimited interviewers, analytics, custom scorecards, API).

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $3K-$8K MRR. The pain is real (bad hires cost $30K+) and the ROI story is strong ("better hiring decisions for $39/mo"). But: startups only hire in bursts (2-3 months on, 2-3 months off), which means high seasonal churn. Annual pricing is critical. Also, the market for "startup hiring tools" is noisy even if the specific "scorecard + debrief" niche is underserved.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: Full-stack Next.js for the app. AI for scorecard generation and debrief summarization. API integration with Google Calendar. Has experience building collaborative tools. Can ship the core flow in 2 weeks.

**Kill criteria**: If < 30 teams create a role in 60 days, or if founders say "we just use Notion" (the Notion template is the real competitor). Also kill if seasonal churn > 20% monthly.

### Idea 3: DocuParse API — Vertical Document Extraction API for Niche Document Types

**Problem**: The big document extraction APIs (AWS Textract, Google Document AI, Mindee) are general-purpose. They handle invoices, receipts, and standard forms well. But they struggle with niche document types: insurance certificates of liability (COIs), lease abstracts, vendor agreement key terms, employment contracts, construction permits, freight bills of lading, etc. Businesses that process these documents in bulk (property managers, insurance brokers, logistics companies, staffing agencies) either do manual data entry or build custom extraction pipelines on top of general APIs — which costs $10K-$50K in engineering time.

The opportunity is a vertical API that specializes in 2-3 niche document types and does them BETTER than general-purpose APIs, offered as a pay-per-document API.

**Who has it**: (1) Property management companies processing lease documents and COIs. (2) Insurance brokers extracting key terms from policies. (3) Staffing agencies parsing employment contracts and offer letters. (4) Construction companies processing permits and compliance docs. Any industry that processes a specific document type repeatedly, at volume, and the general APIs don't handle their format well.

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **AWS Textract / Google Document AI**: General-purpose. Good at invoices and receipts. Mediocre on niche documents — they extract text but don't understand the STRUCTURE of a COI or lease abstract. You still need custom post-processing.
- **Mindee**: Offers pre-built models for invoices, receipts, passports, and a few others. No COI model, no lease model, no employment contract model. Custom model training is available but requires 50+ training samples and technical expertise.
- **Parseur/Parsio**: Email-based extraction. Good for repetitive email-attached documents. Not a developer API.
- **Gap**: A developer-friendly API that ships pre-trained models for niche document types (COIs, leases, employment contracts) with 95%+ accuracy, pay-per-document pricing ($0.10-0.50/doc), and zero setup. The customer uploads a COI → gets back structured JSON with insured party, policy number, coverage types, limits, effective dates, certificate holder, etc.

**MVP scope (3-4 weeks)**:
1. Pick ONE vertical to start: Certificate of Insurance (COI) extraction. COIs are standardized (ACORD forms), high-volume (every contractor sends one to every client), and accuracy matters (wrong coverage limits = liability exposure).
2. Build extraction pipeline: PDF → image → LLM-based extraction (Claude vision or GPT-4o) with structured output schema.
3. REST API: POST a document, GET structured JSON back.
4. Developer portal: docs, API keys, usage dashboard.
5. Stripe metered billing: $0.15/document, first 50 free/month.

**Revenue model**: Pay-per-document: $0.10-0.50/doc depending on complexity. Free tier: 50 docs/month. Pro: $49/mo (includes 500 docs, then $0.08/doc). Enterprise: custom pricing for 10K+/month.

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $5K-$20K MRR IF you nail one vertical. COI processing alone is a $500M+ market — property managers, construction companies, and GCs process thousands of COIs per year. But: the build is harder (3-4 weeks for MVP, accuracy is make-or-break), distribution requires reaching a specific vertical (not general SEO), and you're competing with the long tail of Textract's capability.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: AI/LLM integration is the core product. Python for the extraction pipeline. API design (building production APIs is his day job). TypeScript for the developer portal. Experience with structured data extraction from complex inputs.

**Kill criteria**: If extraction accuracy < 90% on real COIs (trust is everything for an API product), or if < 10 paying customers in 90 days. Also kill if the LLM cost per document exceeds $0.05 (margin squeeze at $0.15/doc pricing).

## Honest Assessment

**None of the Iteration 3 ideas beat ScopeShield (19/25).** All three scored 16/25, below the Iteration 2 top performers. Here's why each falls short:

### DeadlineGuard: Market Is Filling In
The contract renewal reminder space looked empty 2 years ago. It's not anymore. Termedora ($49/mo), ExpiryEdge (free+paid), Contract Hound ($95/mo), and Stitchflow (free) collectively cover most of the space. The "all-in-one + AI extraction" angle is differentiated, but not enough to overcome the existing player density. ExpiryEdge in particular is surprisingly close to this exact idea — it covers licenses, insurance, contracts, certs, and sends multi-channel reminders. The gap is narrower than it appears from the outside.

### HireKit: Seasonal Churn Is the Killer
The structured hiring scorecard + debrief idea is genuinely underserved. No tool does team-based anti-bias scorecards well at the startup tier. But the fundamental economics are bad: startups hire in 2-3 month bursts, then stop. Monthly churn of 15-25% is likely. HireLikeaPro being free (funded by the founder's recruiting firm) further compresses willingness to pay. The TAM of "startups hiring 1-5 people this quarter who want structured scorecards" is real but the RETENTION is the problem.

### DocuParse API: Best Gap, Hardest Build
This has the most defensible market gap — no one offers pre-trained extraction for niche document types like COIs or leases at indie pricing. But it's also the hardest to build (accuracy is make-or-break), the hardest to distribute (vertical-specific B2B marketing), and the most capital-intensive (LLM costs per document eat into margins). It's a great idea for a VC-backed startup; it's risky for a solo builder who needs $2K MRR in 6 months.

### The Honest Takeaway After 3 Iterations

After exploring 9 ideas across 3 iterations (developer tools, creator/freelancer tools, compliance/HR/API tools), the landscape is clear:

1. **ScopeShield (19/25) remains the strongest candidate.** The HoneyBook price hike timing, the tangible ROI story, and the freelancer distribution channels make it the best risk-adjusted bet.

2. **BriefBot and PostPilot (both 18/25) are solid #2 and #3.** BriefBot has higher price point potential; PostPilot is faster to build.

3. **Iteration 3's verticals (compliance, HR, API) didn't surface a killer gap.** The compliance/contract space is more competitive than expected. HR tools for small teams face seasonal churn. API products have great margins but hard distribution.

4. **The pattern across 3 iterations**: the best micro-SaaS ideas target a WORKFLOW (not just a feature), serve a buyer who already pays for tools ($50-200/mo budget), and have a distribution channel that's naturally concentrated (freelancer communities > "small businesses" broadly).

**Recommendation**: Stop iterating on new verticals. ScopeShield is the highest-scoring idea (19/25) and has the best combination of timing, ROI clarity, and distribution. The next step is validation: survey 20 freelancers on scope creep losses, validate willingness to pay $29/mo, and prototype the core flow (brief → proposal → scope tracker → change order).
