# Tool Ideas — Iteration 4 (2026-04-05)

## Focus: E-commerce, physical compliance, niche B2B, legal tools

Explored 15+ fresh categories after StubSnap was killed. Visited actual competitor sites (ProofSnap, Page Vault) per new research protocol.

### Categories Explored & Killed

- **Invoice generators** — QuickBooks, Zoho, Wise all free. 10+ free generators with no signup. Kill filter #1.
- **T-shirt/product mockup generators** — Canva free, Printify free, Mockey AI free (20K+ templates). Kill filter #1.
- **Lien waiver generators** — Built (free), LienShield (free tier), Legal Templates, Jotform, Rocket Lawyer all free. Kill.
- **Certificate of Insurance (COI) tools** — Enterprise SaaS territory ($1K+/yr). Not utility tool shaped.
- **SDS/Safety Data Sheet generators** — Enterprise pricing ($1,495+ for software). Biotech-adjacent. Kill.
- **Certificate generators (training/awards)** — Canva free, Piktochart free, Visme free, 5+ free AI generators. Kill.
- **AI old photo restoration** — 10+ tools, many free (NoteGPT, Imgupscaler, Photobooth). Image tool saturation (pattern #3).
- **Commercial invoice generators** — FedEx/DHL include free with shipping. Free templates everywhere. Kill filter #5.
- **AI product photography** — 20+ tools. Photoroom (250 free/mo), Pebblely (40 free/mo). Prices as low as $0.05/image. Kill.
- **HS code/tariff calculators** — US government free (hts.usitc.gov), Flexport free simulator. Calculator = lead-gen (pattern #8).
- **Embroidery digitizing (AI)** — Free OSS (Ink/Stitch), online converters (Stitchique free), $10 human services. Kill.
- **Virtual staging** — 12+ tools, prices from $0.23/photo. Saturated.
- **Floor plan generators** — 5+ tools, free options (Floor Plan AI, Planner 5D free tier). Kill.
- **OSHA safety sign generators** — Physical product companies (ComplianceSigns, Clarion, SafetySign.com). Online-Sign.com offers FREE printable signs. Not utility tool territory — it's e-commerce with manufacturing.
- **Privacy policy generators** — Termly free, FreePrivacyPolicy free, Enzuzo free, CookieYes free. 12+ competitors. Kill.

### Deep Dive: Legal Web Evidence Capture

**The most promising idea this iteration** — but ultimately killed by existing competitor quality.

**Concept**: URL → certified screenshot with cryptographic hash + blockchain timestamp → court-admissible evidence PDF. Page Vault charges $100/capture (enterprise). Gap between free screenshots and expensive enterprise tools.

**Competitor analysis (VISITED actual sites per new protocol)**:
- **ProofSnap** (getproofsnap.com): Chrome extension. $8.99-28.99/mo OR $4.99 pay-as-you-go (10 captures = $0.50/capture). SHA-256 + RSA-4096 + Bitcoin blockchain timestamps. Video recording of capture. 19 languages. Extensive blog content targeting niche legal queries (divorce evidence, cyberbullying, Airbnb disputes, OSINT). 23 countries. Trusted by State Attorney General's offices, Hogan Lovells.
- **Page Vault**: Enterprise desktop app. Patented remote browsing. Contact-sales pricing. Targets law firms.

**Kill reason**: ProofSnap already fills the gap perfectly — affordable ($0.50/capture), feature-rich (blockchain + video + deepfake detection), extensive SEO content moat, trusted by legal professionals. No meaningful gap to exploit.

## Key Finding: Utility Tool Exhaustion

After 4 iterations exploring **60+ categories**, no idea has scored 16+ (build threshold). The pattern is clear:

1. **Every "generate X" category** has 5-30+ free tools in 2026
2. **AI tools** either have free tiers or subscription pricing that absorbs low-volume users
3. **Compliance-gated tools** (the best pattern found) either have free government alternatives OR existing competitors are full platforms, not simple tools
4. **The gap between "free" and "paid" has narrowed dramatically** — what used to cost $10-50 per use now costs $0-2

### Possible pivot directions for iteration 5:
1. **Multi-tool portfolio strategy** — like SmallPDF or TinyWow, aggregate 20-30 micro-tools under one domain. Revenue from ads + premium tier. Different competitive dynamics (SEO breadth, not depth).
2. **API/developer-facing tools** — sell picks and shovels to OTHER tool builders (but pattern #4 says dev tools trend free).
3. **Non-English-first markets** — find tools with English saturation but gaps in Spanish, Portuguese, etc. (conflicts with English-first constraint but worth discussing).
4. **Physical product integration** — tools where the OUTPUT is a physical product you ship (print-on-demand, custom signage). Different business model.
5. **Abandon utility tools entirely** — the 2026 landscape may simply be too saturated for new transactional tools. Focus on SaaS (APIDelta) and competitive audits instead.
