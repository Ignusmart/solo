# Self-Storage Software Wedge: Adversarial Analysis

**Date**: 2026-04-05  
**Verdict**: SKIP — crowded, well-funded incumbents, switching costs work against you

## Market Size

~63,000 US facilities. ~56-65% independently owned (35,000-41,000). Software market $2.5B, growing 12.7%/yr. Average software spend ~$126/mo entry-level.

## Storable Monopoly — Real but Already Under Attack

Storable owns SiteLink, StorEdge, Easy Storage Solutions, and payment processors (Select Merchant, PAYA). ~30,000 facilities use Storable products. Complaints are loud and real: forced payment processor bundling, price hikes every 1.5-2 years, gutted customer service, double-billing bugs, no phone support. Switching is described as "a nightmare" taking weeks/months — which is exactly why operators stay.

## Competition is Dense

**Full PMS challengers (all funded, all improving):**
- **Cubby** — $63M Series A from Goldman Sachs. 400+ operators, 500K units. Has AI voice agents, revenue management, e-commerce. The real Storable killer.
- **Storeganise** — Open API, 30+ integrations, ~$50/mo for 100 units. Strong with international/complex operators.
- **Hummingbird (Tenant Inc.)** — Built by operators for operators. Full platform.
- **Monument** — Enterprise-grade, open ecosystem, targets REITs/multi-site.
- **Stora** — Clean UI, growth-focused, small-mid operators.
- **Unit Trac** — Built by an owner, ultra-simple.
- **Yardi Store** — Enterprise behemoth, dynamic pricing built in.
- **QuikStor** — Relaunched 2024 with new ownership, new platform.

**Standalone revenue management (the obvious wedge — already taken):**
- **Prorize** — 20 years in market, 8,000+ stores, 300K demand forecasts/day, proven 10%+ revenue lift. The incumbent point solution.
- **Veritec VRMS** — 8,000+ stores, 9-14% revenue increase, cloud-based, no upfront cost.
- **RevMan AI (White Label Storage)** — New entrant, AI-powered, integrates with existing PMS.

## Why There Is No Wedge

1. **Rate management is the obvious standalone tool — and Prorize/Veritec already own it** with 8,000+ stores each and 20 years of data.
2. **Cubby ($63M) is building the full Storable replacement** with modern stack + AI. They are 2-3 years ahead of any new entrant.
3. **Switching costs cut both ways** — operators hate Storable but also won't adopt your tool if it requires any migration. A point solution must integrate with SiteLink API, which Storable controls.
4. **The TAM for a point solution is small** — 35K independent facilities x $50-100/mo = $21-42M ceiling, competing against funded players for every dollar.
5. **Domain expertise barrier** — storage operators are not tech-forward. Sales cycles are long, trust is earned through industry conferences (SSA) and word-of-mouth, not PLG.
