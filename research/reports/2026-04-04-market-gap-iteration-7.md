### Idea 1: AIComply Lite -- EU AI Act Risk Inventory & Compliance Tracker for SMEs

**Problem**: The EU AI Act becomes fully applicable on August 2, 2026 -- 4 months from now. Every company that develops, deploys, or uses AI systems serving EU users must: (1) inventory all AI systems, (2) classify each by risk tier (prohibited, high, limited, minimal), (3) document compliance for high-risk systems per Annex IV requirements, and (4) maintain ongoing monitoring. Most SMEs (50-500 employees) have not started. They use AI tools (chatbots, recommendation engines, hiring filters, content generators) but have no formal inventory or risk classification. The penalty for non-compliance is up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global annual turnover.

**Who has it**: SaaS companies, e-commerce businesses, HR tech firms, fintech companies -- any SME with 5-50 AI-powered features/tools that needs to comply before August 2026. Estimated 100K+ EU SMEs in scope.

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **EuroComply** (EUR 1,788/yr = ~EUR 149/mo): The direct competitor. Covers 5 EU regulations. Uses official Annex III criteria. But the price is steep for a small startup just needing AI Act compliance. Also bundles NIS2, CRA, Data Act -- overkill for a company that only needs AI Act inventory + classification.
- **EU AI Act Compliance Checker** (free, artificialintelligenceact.eu): Useful for a single system check but no inventory management, no tracking, no team collaboration, no documentation generation. A one-time quiz, not an ongoing compliance tool.
- **Enactia, Scytale, Sprinto**: Enterprise GRC platforms ($5K-50K/yr). Way too expensive and complex for SMEs.
- **Law firms/consultants**: EUR 10K-50K for a compliance audit. Out of reach for most SMEs.
- **Gap**: No tool at $29-49/mo focuses ONLY on the EU AI Act for small teams. EuroComply at EUR 149/mo is 3-5x too expensive for a 10-person startup with 8 AI features. The free checker is too basic. There's a genuine price gap between "free quiz" and "EUR 1,788/yr platform."

**MVP scope (2 weeks)**:
1. AI System Inventory: catalog each AI system (name, vendor, purpose, data inputs, who it affects)
2. Risk Classification Wizard: guided questionnaire based on Annex III criteria to classify each system (prohibited/high/limited/minimal)
3. Compliance Dashboard: traffic-light view of all AI systems with risk tier, compliance status, deadline countdown
4. Documentation Templates: pre-structured Annex IV documentation drafts for high-risk systems
5. Action Items: auto-generated checklist per system based on risk tier (e.g., "high-risk: complete conformity assessment, register in EU database, implement human oversight")
6. Team collaboration: assign systems to team members, track completion
7. Stripe billing

**Revenue model**: Free tier (3 AI systems, basic classification). Pro $39/mo (unlimited systems, documentation templates, team collaboration, deadline tracking). Business $79/mo (audit trail, export for regulators, priority support).

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $5K-15K MRR. The timing is exceptional -- 4 months until the deadline. 100K+ SMEs in scope. The ROI is existential (avoid EUR 35M fines). But: (a) the window is narrow -- after Aug 2026, urgency drops, (b) regulatory compliance has high churn once companies pass the deadline, (c) EuroComply could drop pricing, (d) the EU may extend deadlines or soften enforcement for SMEs.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: Full-stack TS/Python for the platform. AI/LLM skills for the risk classification wizard (can use RAG on the actual AI Act text to power smart classification). Data pipeline experience for audit trail/reporting. Regulatory domain knowledge from Web3 compliance adjacent work.

**Kill criteria**: If EuroComply launches a sub-EUR 50/mo tier. If the EU extends the Aug 2026 deadline. If fewer than 50 signups in 30 days. If churn exceeds 20%/mo after August 2026.

### Idea 2: MenuMath -- Simple Menu Profitability Calculator for Independent Restaurants

**Problem**: Independent restaurant owners (1-3 locations) don't know which menu items are profitable. They price by "feeling" or competitor matching, not by calculating food cost percentage, contribution margin, and popularity. Menu engineering (categorizing items as stars/plowhorses/puzzles/dogs) can boost profits 10-15%, but the existing tools either require POS integration (Eatos $49/mo), are enterprise-grade (Fourth, Apicbase), or are Excel-based (EZchef, FoodCost Chef). A restaurant owner who just wants to plug in recipe costs and sales data to see "drop this item, raise this price, promote this dish" doesn't have a simple, standalone web tool for this.

**Who has it**: Independent restaurant owners (500K+ in the US alone). Specifically the 70% who still price by gut feeling and don't use menu engineering software.

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **Eatos** ($49-199/mo): Requires POS integration (Toast, Square). If you don't use a supported POS, you can't use Eatos. Many independents use paper tickets or unsupported systems.
- **Fourth, Apicbase, MarketMan**: Enterprise/mid-market ($200-1000/mo). Overkill for a single-location restaurant.
- **EZchef, FoodCost Chef**: Excel-based. No web interface, no mobile access, no collaborative features. Better than nothing but clunky.
- **Free calculators** (Supy, CookKeepBook, Miss Vickie): Single-item calculators. No menu-wide analysis, no star/dog matrix, no pricing recommendations.
- **Gap (thin)**: The gap is between "free single-item calculator" and "Eatos at $49/mo requiring POS integration." A standalone web tool at $19-29/mo that lets you input recipes + sales data manually (no POS required) and generates a menu engineering matrix with pricing recommendations could serve POS-less restaurants. But the question is: will a restaurant owner who doesn't use a POS pay $19/mo for a web tool?

**MVP scope (1-2 weeks)**:
1. Add menu items: name, category, recipe ingredients with costs, portion size
2. Add sales data: units sold per item per period (manual input or CSV)
3. Auto-calculate: food cost %, contribution margin, sales mix %
4. Menu engineering matrix: auto-classify items as stars/plowhorses/puzzles/dogs
5. AI-powered recommendations: "raise price on [item] by $2," "consider removing [item]," "promote [item] more prominently"
6. Simple dashboard: total food cost %, top/bottom performers, recommended actions
7. Stripe billing

**Revenue model**: Free tier (10 menu items, basic costing). Pro $19/mo (unlimited items, menu engineering matrix, AI recommendations, multiple menus). Business $39/mo (multi-location, team access, historical trend tracking).

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $2K-5K MRR. The TAM is massive (500K+ independent restaurants in US) but: (a) restaurant owners are notoriously low-tech and price-sensitive, (b) $19/mo competes with free Excel templates and single-item calculators, (c) the "manual input" requirement (no POS integration) is both the differentiator and the weakness -- data entry is friction, (d) churn will be high as restaurants are seasonal and cash-strapped.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: Full-stack web app. AI for pricing recommendations. Data visualization. But this is a commodity build -- no deep technical moat.

**Kill criteria**: If fewer than 50 signups in 60 days. If restaurant owners say "I just use a spreadsheet." If Eatos drops pricing below $29/mo or removes POS requirement. If monthly churn exceeds 15%.

### Idea 3: DocPulse -- AI Documentation Health Monitor (Standalone, Platform-Agnostic)

**Problem**: Engineering teams ship faster than they document. Docs go stale within weeks. Support tickets rise 23% when knowledge bases decay. But doc staleness detection has historically been tied to specific platforms (GitHub-only, or bundled into documentation platforms). A team using Notion for internal docs, GitBook for external docs, and Confluence for runbooks has no unified view of doc health across all three.

**Who has it**: Engineering teams (10-100 people) at growing startups that use 2-3 documentation tools. Tech leads and documentation owners who get blamed when docs are wrong.

**Why current solutions fail**:
- **Doctective**: Watches GitHub PRs, auto-fixes docs. GitHub-centric. Doesn't cover Notion, Confluence, or GitBook docs that aren't code-linked.
- **Rasepi**: Full documentation platform with trust scores. You have to migrate your docs TO Rasepi. Doesn't work as a layer on top of existing tools.
- **Fiberplane Drift**: CI linter. Only works for docs anchored to code specs. Doesn't detect staleness in user-facing docs, runbooks, or internal wikis.
- **Ferndesk/Fern Agent**: Focused on help centers. Connects to GitHub, Intercom, Zendesk. Not for internal documentation.
- **Mintlify Workflows**: Drafts doc PRs from code changes. Only for Mintlify-hosted docs.
- **Gap**: Every staleness detection tool is platform-specific or requires migration. No tool sits on TOP of existing documentation platforms (Notion + GitBook + Confluence) and provides a unified health score with staleness alerts. The "documentation observability layer" doesn't exist.

**BUT -- and this is critical**: This is exactly the DocDrift concept from Iteration 1 (scored 17/25), and the space has gotten MUCH more competitive since then. Five new entrants (Doctective, Rasepi, Fiberplane Drift, Ferndesk, Mintlify Workflows) have arrived in the last 12 months. The "platform-agnostic overlay" angle is differentiated, but:
- Building integrations for Notion + GitBook + Confluence + GitHub is a 4-6 week effort, not 1-2 weeks
- The staleness detection heuristics (last edited, link rot, terminology drift, readership drop) are complex to get right
- The buyers (engineering managers, doc owners) are hard to reach and have intermittent pain

**MVP scope (3-4 weeks -- already over the 1-2 week bar)**:
1. Connect to Notion, GitBook, Confluence via APIs
2. Index all documents, track last-edited dates, link health, terminology consistency
3. AI-powered staleness detection: flag docs that reference outdated features, deprecated APIs, or changed terminology
4. Unified dashboard: health score per doc, per team, per platform
5. Alerts: Slack/email notification when doc health drops below threshold
6. Suggested updates: AI-generated fix suggestions for stale docs

**Revenue model**: Free tier (1 platform, 50 docs). Pro $29/mo (3 platforms, 500 docs, AI suggestions). Team $59/mo (unlimited, team dashboard, Slack alerts).

**Realistic MRR ceiling**: $3K-8K MRR. The pain is real (23% more support tickets from stale docs) but intermittent. Teams notice it during onboarding or incidents, not daily. The competition has intensified significantly -- 5 new entrants in 12 months means the market is being served, just in fragmented ways.

**Why Jobelo's skills give an edge**: API integration experience (multiple platform APIs), AI/NLP for staleness detection, data pipeline for document indexing. This is genuinely technical -- more moat than most ideas analyzed.

**Kill criteria**: If Notion/Confluence add native staleness detection. If any of the 5 competitors go platform-agnostic. If the build exceeds 4 weeks. If churn exceeds 15%/mo.

## Honest Assessment

### AIComply Lite (16/25) Is the Best Idea This Iteration -- But Still 3 Points Below ScopeShield

AIComply Lite has the strongest timing signal of any idea across all 7 iterations. The EU AI Act deadline (August 2, 2026) creates genuine urgency. The penalty structure (up to EUR 35M) makes the ROI existential. The price gap between "free checker" and "EUR 149/mo EuroComply" is real.

But it has structural weaknesses:
1. **Deadline-driven demand = cliff churn.** Once companies pass the August deadline, the urgency evaporates. Monthly churn will spike after Q3 2026.
2. **EU-only market.** Unlike ScopeShield (global freelancer market), AIComply targets only EU companies or companies serving EU users. Smaller TAM.
3. **Regulatory expertise required.** Getting the risk classification logic right is essential -- mistakes create liability. Jobelo has adjacent compliance experience but is not an EU AI Act specialist.
4. **EuroComply could price-cut.** At EUR 1,788/yr, EuroComply has plenty of room to launch a EUR 39/mo starter tier and eliminate the gap.

### MenuMath (12/25) and DocPulse (13/25) Are Below the Bar

MenuMath targets restaurant owners who are offline-first, price-sensitive, and hard to reach. The "no POS required" angle is a feature, not a product. Eatos at $49/mo is already affordable enough for restaurants that care about menu engineering.

DocPulse is DocDrift (Iter 1) revisited. The space has gotten MORE competitive, not less. Five new entrants in 12 months. The "platform-agnostic" angle requires 3-4 weeks to build and ongoing API maintenance. Not viable as a 1-2 week MVP.

### DocDrift Downgrade Note

DocDrift was scored 17/25 in Iteration 1. Based on the competitive landscape update (Doctective, Rasepi, Fiberplane Drift, Ferndesk, Mintlify Workflows -- all launched since Iter 1), DocDrift's Competition score should drop from 3 to 2, bringing its total to **16/25**. This does not affect the top 5 ranking.
