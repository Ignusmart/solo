## What are you building, and where are you at?

Two products, both pre-revenue. APIDelta (apidelta.dev) crawls third-party API changelogs (Stripe, Twilio, OpenAI, etc.) and classifies breaking changes with AI, alerting engineering teams at B2B SaaS companies before customer complaints catch them. Community launch April 15 — Day 9 numbers are weak (~13 visitors/7d, 0 trial signups, hard kill checkpoint May 15). ScanAble is a parallel pay-per-scan ADA/WCAG accessibility tool ($19/domain) targeting SMBs at risk of compliance lawsuits, with Google Ads running since April 10. I'm building solo from Bogotá — Principal Engineer, 10+ years (Solidity + AI + TS/Python), async-only by constraint.

## What's the hardest thing you're working through in your business right now?

My natural failure mode: when distribution is hard, I retreat into research. APIDelta is 9 days into launch with 13 visitors and 0 signups. Last night I ran 26 iterations of market-gap analysis trying to find a "better" idea — the 26th iteration confirmed what the 1st did: ideas aren't the bottleneck. The hardest thing right now is the discipline of staying with the two products I have and learning distribution as a non-natural skill — specifically, how to show up in engineering communities authentically as a builder, not a poster, without it feeling like a marketing stunt. That's the gap between me and the founders I admire: they've each done a 90-day commitment to one channel and figured out what actually moves people. I keep hopping, or hiding behind more research. That's the part I'd most benefit from being around people who've done it.
