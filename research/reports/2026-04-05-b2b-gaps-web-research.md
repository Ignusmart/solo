# B2B Software Gaps & Underserved Categories -- Web Research (2026-04-05)

## 1. New G2 Categories (March 2026)

G2 added 14 new categories. Notable ones: **AI Gateways** (middleware for multi-model routing, caching, API key mgmt), **Conversational Interface Agents** (agentic layer between users and enterprise systems), and **Answer Engine Optimization** (AEO -- optimizing for AI-powered search). These signal real buyer demand outpacing existing tooling.

- **Gap**: AI Gateway tooling is nascent; most enterprises rig custom solutions. Few indie-scale products exist.
- **Buyer**: Engineering teams at mid-market companies using multiple LLM providers. $200-$2K/mo.
- **Competition**: Early -- mostly VC-backed (Portkey, Helicone). Room for lightweight/self-hosted alternatives.

## 2. Expensive Tools People Are Fleeing

**Jira**: Aggressive Cloud price increases + "Maximum Quantity Billing" (charges peak user count, no refunds). Self-hosted alternatives (Plane, Redmine) gaining traction. Dozens of "alternatives to Jira" articles in 2026.

**Salesforce**: 60-70% cost savings cited when switching to HubSpot, Pipedrive ($12.50/user), or Less Annoying CRM ($15/user). The shift is toward "AI-first CRMs that don't require a dedicated admin."

- **Gap**: Vertical-specific lightweight project trackers (not another generic PM tool). E.g., project management for agencies, construction, legal -- domain-tailored, not Jira-lite.
- **Buyer**: Teams of 5-50 paying $7-$30/user/mo. SMBs specifically.
- **Competition**: Crowded for generic alternatives; thin for vertical-specific.

## 3. What's Actually Making Money (Indie/Micro SaaS)

Market projected $15.7B to $59.6B by 2030. Solo founders hitting $5K-$50K MRR. Real examples: Plausible Analytics ($3.1M ARR, privacy-first analytics), Papermark ($45K MRR, document sharing/tracking).

**Profitable niches**: Content repurposing tools ($19-$99/mo), niche scheduling ($29-$149/mo), vertical CRMs for specific professions (fitness coaches, photographers). Key: 100 customers x $99/mo = ~$10K MRR.

- **Gap**: Content repurposing still fragmented -- podcast-to-shorts, blog-to-social, video-to-docs. No dominant solo-founder tool.
- **Competition**: Moderate. Many AI wrappers but high churn and low switching costs.

## 4. Vertical SaaS Underserved Industries

**Laundry/garment care**: 90K+ US retail locations, almost zero modern tech infrastructure. Cents just raised $140M for this space -- validates demand but also shows it's now being addressed at scale.

**Specialized medical practices**: Dermatology, physiotherapy, etc. have workflows generic EMR systems ignore. Niche SaaS is defensible here.

**Construction, logistics, waste management**: Late digital adopters. Still running on spreadsheets and phone calls.

- **Gap**: Operational software for trades/field services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) -- scheduling, invoicing, job costing. Existing tools (ServiceTitan) are expensive and enterprise-focused.
- **Buyer**: Small trade businesses, 1-20 employees, willing to pay $50-$200/mo.
- **Competition**: ServiceTitan dominates upmarket; indie opportunity is in the $50-$100/mo tier for micro-businesses.

## 5. Failed/Shut-Down SaaS (2025-2026) -- Where the Problem Was Real

B2B SaaS shutdowns rose from 5.2% to 7.7%. Most failures are 2021-2022 vintage: **AI wrappers/copilots** (low margins, no moat, API cost shifts), **creator economy platforms**, and **first-gen fintech experiments**.

**Cydoc** (health AI, 7 years): Shut down Aug 2025. Founder said deploying health AI is 20% tech, 80% workflow integration and sales infrastructure. The problem (clinical documentation) is real but execution requires deep domain embedding.

- **Signal**: Thin-wrapper AI products are dying. Products that embed deeply into a specific workflow and own the data layer survive.
- **Opportunity**: Rebuild failed AI-wrapper concepts as workflow-native tools with proprietary data moats (not just an API call to GPT).

## 6. Reddit Frustrations -- Where B2B Software Still Fails

Top complaints: **silent integration failures** (inventory syncs break with no alert), **months-long deployment** for Zendesk/Salesforce, **no Slack/Teams-native support**, **opaque pricing with hidden setup fees**, **AI chatbots giving wrong answers** (41% cite inaccuracy).

- **Gap**: Integration monitoring / "data pipeline health checks" for non-technical teams. Also: transparent, instant-deploy B2B support tools that live inside Slack.
- **Buyer**: Ops teams at 20-200 person companies. $50-$300/mo.
- **Competition**: Plain.com entering this space; otherwise thin.

## Summary: Top 3 Actionable Gaps

1. **AI Gateway / LLM proxy for small teams** -- model routing, cost tracking, caching. Self-hostable. G2 just created the category.
2. **Vertical project management for a specific trade** -- not generic PM, but tailored to agencies, trades, or legal. Undercut Jira on price and complexity.
3. **Integration health monitoring** -- alerts when Zapier/webhook/API syncs silently fail. Non-technical UI. Reddit is full of horror stories.
