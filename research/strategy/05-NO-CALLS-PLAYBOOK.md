# The No-Calls Playbook

How to make money as a developer while minimizing synchronous human interaction.

Brutally reviewed 10 times. Generic platitudes removed. What's left is practical.

---

## Principles (Honest Version)

1. **Sell products, not time** — but accept that products take months to generate income. You may need to sell time (audits, writing) while building.
2. **Async-first, not async-only** — being absolutist about "zero calls ever" caps your earning ceiling. Some high-value opportunities (a $20K audit engagement, a $5K consulting gig) are worth one 30-minute call. Have a threshold.
3. **Self-serve by default** — if someone needs to talk to you to buy, your product has a problem. But for B2B or high-ticket, some async interaction is normal.
4. **Distribution before product** — nobody buys what nobody sees. Start building audience NOW, before you have something to sell. This is the part most developers skip.
5. **Focus beats optionality** — 2 income streams executed well > 5 income streams half-assed.

---

## Your Call Threshold (Fill This In)

Not all synchronous interaction is equal. Define your boundaries:

- **Hard no**: Regular standup meetings, weekly client calls, onboarding calls for < $100 products
- **Maybe**: One-time 30min call for a deal worth > $____
- **Exception**: If someone offers > $____ for a project, a single scoping call is worth it

Having an explicit threshold prevents both extremes: saying yes to everything OR leaving real money on the table.

## Revenue Streams by Interaction Level

### Zero Interaction (passive after creation)
- Digital products on Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy
- Course sales on own platform
- Affiliate links in technical content

**Reality**: These require an audience to generate meaningful income. Without 1K+ email subscribers or 5K+ Twitter followers, expect < $100/month initially.

### Text-Only Interaction (async but active)
- Competitive audit contests (Code4rena, Sherlock)
- Technical articles for company blogs
- Open source + GitHub Sponsors
- Premium SaaS with email support

**Reality**: This is where most of your income will come from initially. Audits and writing trade time for money, but async.

### Strategic Sync (rare, high-value only)
- One-off consulting call for > $500
- Scoping call for a > $5K engagement
- Podcast/interview appearance (builds audience)

**Reality**: Refusing all sync interaction is a luxury you earn after passive income covers your expenses. Until then, be pragmatic.

## How to Decline Calls (Without Burning Bridges)

**When someone asks for a call:**
> "I work async to stay focused on deep work. I find written exchanges lead to better outcomes — can you share your questions via email? I'll respond within 24 hours with a detailed answer."

**When a client insists on a demo:**
> "Here's a 5-minute walkthrough I recorded: [Loom link]. If it raises questions, I'll address them in writing within 24h."

**When someone wants to "pick your brain":**
> "I've written about this here: [link]. For specific questions, I offer async consulting — send me the details and I'll quote a flat rate for a written deliverable."

**When the opportunity is genuinely worth a call:**
> Accept it. One 30-minute call for a $5K+ opportunity is rational, not a failure of your principles.

## Weekly Routine (Realistic Version)

The original routine was too rigid. Here's a more honest version:

### If you need income NOW (first 3-6 months):
| Time Block | Allocation | Activity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| 60% of time | Audits | Competitive audit contests (this is your primary income) |
| 20% of time | Product | Build ONE product (template or MCP servers) |
| 15% of time | Content | Write 1 post/week (builds audience for product launch) |
| 5% of time | Admin | Respond to GitHub issues, emails, check metrics |

**Audit contests run 1-4 weeks. During an active contest, auditing should get 80%+ of your time.**

### After product generates > $1K/mo:
| Time Block | Allocation | Activity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| 40% of time | Audits | Maintain audit income |
| 30% of time | Product | Improve product, add features |
| 20% of time | Content | Write 2 posts/week, engage on Twitter |
| 10% of time | Admin | Support, metrics, planning |

### After passive income covers expenses:
| Time Block | Allocation | Activity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| 20% of time | Audits | Only contests in your domain |
| 40% of time | Product | Scale what works, kill what doesn't |
| 30% of time | Content | Build audience and authority |
| 10% of time | Explore | New ideas, learning |

## The Audience Problem (Most Important Section)

**Honest truth**: Every product idea in your list fails without distribution. You currently don't have a large audience. This section is about fixing that.

### Minimum Viable Audience Before Product Launch
- 500+ Twitter/X followers in your niche
- OR 200+ email subscribers
- OR 3+ articles with 1K+ views each
- OR a popular open source repo (100+ stars)

**If you don't have this, building a product is premature.** Build audience first.

### How to Build Developer Audience (Async-Only)

**Week 1-4**: Foundation
- Pick ONE platform (Twitter/X is best for dev tools)
- Post 3-5x/week about what you're learning from audits
- Share 1 technical insight per day (even short ones)
- Engage with 5-10 relevant posts daily (comments, not just likes)

**Week 5-12**: Authority
- Publish 1 in-depth technical post per week (blog + Twitter thread)
- Share audit contest results (what you found, lessons learned)
- Open source one tool or utility and post about it

**Week 13+**: Launch Readiness
- You should have 300-500 engaged followers
- Test product ideas: "I'm thinking of building X. Would you pay for it?"
- Pre-launch to your audience before going wide

### Content Ideas From Your Actual Experience
- "What I learned auditing Lido's dual governance contracts"
- "5 gas optimization patterns I found in DeFi vault code"
- "Building MCP servers: 3 mistakes I made and how to fix them"
- "From Sails.js to Next.js: migrating a 5-year-old codebase"
- "How Foundry's fuzzing caught a bug I missed manually"

These are content only YOU can write because you actually did the work.

## Burnout Prevention

Running multiple income streams solo is exhausting. Plan for it.

- **One active project at a time**. Not two products + audits + writing + marketing simultaneously.
- **Weekly review (30 min)**: Is what I'm doing this week moving the needle? If not, stop and refocus.
- **Monthly kill review**: Is each income stream worth continuing? Cut ruthlessly.
- **Energy management**: Auditing is mentally draining. Don't schedule product work on the same day as deep audit sessions.
- **Take Sundays fully off**. No "just checking metrics." Rest compounds.

## Metrics to Track

### Leading Indicators (track weekly)
- [ ] Hours spent on audit contests this week
- [ ] Number of technical posts published this week
- [ ] Twitter/X follower count
- [ ] Email subscriber count
- [ ] GitHub stars on open source projects

### Lagging Indicators (track monthly)
- [ ] Total income this month (audits + products + writing)
- [ ] Number of product sales
- [ ] Audit contest payouts received
- [ ] Hours spent in synchronous communication (goal: < 2 hours/month)

### Kill Triggers
- If a product makes < $200 in its first 60 days after launch: reassess positioning or kill it
- If audit contests average < $500/month after 3 months: invest in better tooling or pick different contest types
- If content gets < 100 views per post after 8 weeks: change platform or format
